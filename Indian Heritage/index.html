Some Cool Stories

The Legend of the Iron Pillar of Delhi: The Iron Pillar of Delhi, standing tall in the Qutub Complex, is a marvel of ancient Indian metallurgy. Erected by King Chandragupta II in the 4th century CE, this 7-meter-high column has baffled scientists and historians alike due to its rust-resistant composition. Despite being over 1,600 years old and exposed to the harsh elements, the pillar has remained remarkably free of corrosion. This engineering feat highlights the advanced knowledge and skill in metalwork that existed in ancient India, making it a testament to the ingenuity and craftsmanship of its creators.

The Mystical City of Varanasi: Varanasi, also known as Kashi or Benares, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, Varanasi is considered the spiritual capital of India. According to Hindu mythology, the city was founded by Lord Shiva, and it has been a center of learning, culture, and devotion for thousands of years. Pilgrims flock to Varanasi to perform rituals and cleanse their sins in the holy waters of the Ganges. The city's narrow lanes, vibrant ghats, and the daily Ganga Aarti ceremony create a unique spiritual atmosphere that encapsulates the eternal essence of Indian heritage and spirituality.
